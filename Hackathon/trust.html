Trust

Trust is an integral aspect of a healthy relationship, but for a lot of different reasons, it is something many people struggle with.

What does trust mean? Trusting someone means you think they are trustworthy, you trust them and you feel physically and emotionally secure with them. Trust is something two persons can build together in a relationship when they decide to trust each other. You can't say or show confidence; trusting someone is a choice you make.

How Do I Build Mutual Trust in a Relationship?

In a stable partnership building trust is something that happens slowly. What do you know if you like somebody? That can be a difficult question to answer, particularly at the start of a relationship. Two important things to consider when making that decision are your own feelings about a person and the way they act over time.

It is of course necessary for both partners to trust and be trusted in a healthy relationship, to open up and be vulnerable to one another. Trust can not be created if there is only one partner able to do that and the other is not. It takes shared effort to create confidence. Only ask yourself, as the relationship progresses:

Have we ever been there for one another?

If there is trust, spouses are not only physically but also emotionally there for each other. Is your partner listening to you and giving you support? Were they open to your issues, your worries and your fears? Will they show compassion and have sincere concern for you? A trustworthy person is capable of displaying empathy and caring for others. It also means they trust you to do what's best for themselves. A partner who tells you that they know best, or that you don't really know how you feel, doesn't show they trust you.

It's also important to bear in mind that in a healthy relationship, you can trust your partner will not respond in a way that threatens your safety or hurts you, no matter what happens. All wants to be in a relationship with someone who has a safe , respectful way of resolving disputes.

Are we still being consistent?

Every person in a relationship shows their trustworthiness by being consistent in their acts. The first habits that you look at may be fairly low, like turning up at agreed-upon times for dates. Holding private information between the two of you and maintaining limits even though one of you isn't pleased are other signs someone is trustworthy. Yet again, learning these things in a relationship happens slowly, when both of you prove that you are consistent in your acts not only sometimes, but all the time.

Can we all tell what we think, and do we say?

Another way a person demonstrates that they are comfortable is when their words and actions match. Of example, if someone says they love you, and then they behave abusively towards you, they don't suit their words and behavior. You don't hurt someone because you love them.

Tackling Jealousy

Usually, envy occurs when one partner feels threatened, or loses faith in the other. Feeling jealous sometimes is common, but what decides whether relationship habits are good, unhealthy or abusive is how a person deals with his or her jealous feelings. Checking in constantly, asking where the other partner is at all times and/or trying to control who is spending time with a partner is not a healthy reaction to jealousy. Those are signs that a partnership lacks confidence. A person does not feel a need to track or control his partner when there is confidence. We don't need to "prove" their love and fidelity to their partner.When you trust someone, you trust them who they're spending time with or where they're going.

Since there are so many different ways to overcome feelings of jealousy, we want to break down some of the common issues surrounding jealousy and/or lack of confidence:

To have connections or to connect with others outside the relationship

All partners should allow one another in a stable relationship to have friends of either gender outside the relationship. It's important to set boundaries and make sure that both of you are on the same page on what might be cheating, but you and your partner should feel encouraged and motivated to have connections with others.

When your partner tells you that you are not allowed to talk to anyone because they might be interested in you, then your partner does not trust you. If one person wants to tell the other who to talk to, what to wear or where to go, they exert dominance and influence, which is dangerous and can also become violent.

Go through a partner's mobile, email and social media to ensure they don't cheat

It's never appropriate to go through your partner 's phone, email or social media without their permission, even though you think your partner might be cheating. In a healthy relationship, if you're worried your partner might be cheating or behaving in a way that doesn't honor your relationship, it 's important to openly communicate your feelings with them. Recall that treating this as a dialogue is more successful than an accusation.

Building up trust after cheating

It's normal to get hurt by cheating or dishonesty, but using the past against your partner isn't cool. Trust is something that we agree to give, and not something that can be won back. It's not fair to them or to you to trust your partner not. When you're unsure whether you can trust your partner or not, then think about what it will take you to trust them again. If your reaction has something to do with checking them up or trying to monitor their behaviour, then you don't trust them really. Instead, strive to change your own feelings of envy and behaviour, and you might be able to get a good relationship going on.When you find like you are unable to trust your partner again, then it might be time to consider whether the partnership is right for you.
