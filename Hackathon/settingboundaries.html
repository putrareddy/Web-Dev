Setting Boundaries

A healthy relationship begins with mutual respect and includes respect for the mental , physical and digital boundaries of each other. Setting boundaries in a relationship can be an on-going process. It 's crucial for partners to recognize and be prepared to accept each other's needs, boundaries, preferences and feelings. People and relationships grow, and everyone has the right to change their boundaries or alter them as they see fit. Having open boundary discussions in a partnership will help to ensure the expectations between both parties are still respected. Here are some useful questions to ask while considering setting limits for your relationship:

Will every partner get the space they need as individuals to lead healthier lives?

Just as nice as having to spend time with your partner, it's also important to have some time away from each other. It is not safe for any partner to attempt to set boundaries or to use guilt or coercion to monitor where their partner is going or with whom they are spending time. Everyone will feel free to spend time alone or with friends and family without having to get their partner 's permission or check in and justify their whereabouts. If boundaries around personal space are not respected, this may be a sign that either or both partners are having confidence issues. Read more about maintaining faith in healthy relationships here.

Is intimacy comfortable and consensual at all times?

In a partnership, sexual consent is completely necessary if you're just starting to date or have been married for years. Sex should never feel necessary and you should always know your partner is concerned about your happiness and boundaries. Both have different experiences, preferences, and levels of comfort when it comes to intimacy, age, and defensive methods. It's important to feel comfortable sharing your intimacy boundaries and trust your partner will always value them.

This can help to chat about limits and desires about sex with your partner when you're in the moment, as well as chat about how you'd like to connect with each other at the moment and make sure you 're still mindful of each other's limits all along. Although setting boundaries in advance can help, you still have the right to set boundaries or change your mind even in the moment. People's levels of comfort and desire change, so it should never be assumed they'll always be okay with it just because someone was okay with something in the past. No matter how long you have been with someone, or how many times you have done it, at any moment you have the right to say no

Is there mutual respect for privacy?

Everybody has the right to privacy and it is not something that you would give up in order to be in a relationship. Although it's okay to share personal information such as passwords to social media, bank accounts, email , phone, etc. if you want to, it should never feel necessary and keeping those private is completely fair. Getting access to personal accounts or information from someone also does not grant someone the right to look at them without permission from the owner. Even if your partner has exchanged passwords, you have every right to expect them to respect your privacy and boundaries. It's never an invitation to leave your private accounts open or violate your privacy.

Should you and your partner value each other's values without being frustrated or feeling bad about each other?

As we have said, everybody is entitled to set limits. Just feel confident expressing your expectations to your partner without having to worry about how they will respond. During a siege, personal boundaries shouldn't feel like castle walls. When you've set limits, you shouldn't feel like you're having to constantly justify or repeat them to make your partner accept them, and vice versa. All people want their partner to feel happy, secure and relaxed in a stable relationship and use knowledge of each other's boundaries to help them understand how to keep the relationship happy and safe.It is not polite or safe to use coercion, make you feel bad, or disagree with you about whether your limits are fair. If you don't feel relaxed or confident setting limits, or your partner doesn't respect your limits, this can be a red flag for dysfunctional or abusive relationship dynamics. Read more about establishing boundaries in a relationship on our website of loveisrespect.

If you answered “yes” to all of these questions, congratulations! It sounds like there are healthy boundaries in your relationship. If you answered “no” to one or more questions, this could be an indication that you and your partner might want to work on creating more boundaries in your relationship, or that you might want to assess for red flags for unhealthy or abusive dynamics in the relationship.
