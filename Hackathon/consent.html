Consent

Consent Is A Mutual Aggrement Between Partners About What They Want To Experience

The expression "no means no" doesn't really offer a full image when it comes to consent, since it puts the burden on one person to refuse or agree. There are still so many different ways in which people interact that they don't feel comfortable with anything (like freezing up or just moving away). When thinking about what consent actually means, the phrase "yes means yes" can be encouraging and useful: consent is a safe, transparent and ongoing dialogue about what both parties are comfortable with and actively want to do together.

Consent Needs To Happen Everytime

Consent means both parties feel free to let their partner know if they do not feel comfortable with anything. Consent can be seen as a phase, something that has to be continuously created. Only because in the past you have consented to an activity doesn't mean you can agree to that activity from now on. In a healthy relationship, you are always entitled to set and change your own limits depending on what actually feels good to you.

Your Relationship Status Doesnit Make Consent Automatic

If it's the first or hundredth time, a hook-up, a romantic relationship or a marriage, no one is ever forced to give permission, even though they've done so in the past. You are the only one to ever own your body.

It’s Not Consent If, For Any Reason, You're Afraid Or Unable To Say No

When you are being coerced, under pressure or pressured to say yes, it is not consent. This is therefore not consent if you or a friend are unable to give legal consent, which involves being intoxicated, unconscious or under the influence of conscious altering substances such as alcohol, other prescription medications and other drugs.

Consent Is Not A Free Pass

Saying yes to one act does not mean that you are under no obligation to commit to any other act. Any act of sexual contact demands its own approval. If you're in the midst of something and feel uncomfortable, or if things go beyond what you feel right at that moment, you're always entitled to stop, even if you agreed earlier on.

There Is No Such Thing As Implied Consent

Flirting with others, chatting, showing interest or other acts don't equal consent. Therefore when all parties freely, specifically and actively decide to partake in what is happening does consent occur.

Stop

When someone involved is not consenting, then rape, sexual harassment or violence is or may be what is happening.
