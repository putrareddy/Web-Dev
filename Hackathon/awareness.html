Awareness

Domestic violence (also called intimate partner violence, domestic abuse, or relationship abuse) is a pattern of actions one person uses in an interpersonal relationship to retain power and control over another person. At any point in a relationship it can happen to anyone. Domestic violence involves actions that injure physically, trigger anxiety, hinder a person from doing what they want, or compel them to act in ways they don't want. Like everyone so far this October, we are raising awareness of domestic abuse through the # 1Thing movement and celebrating DVAM.

DVAM :

The Domestic Violence Awareness Month (DVAM) is celebrated during October as a tool to bring together activists around the nation to end domestic violence. Across the world, neighborhoods and community groups will link with each other in a true sense of unity to end domestic violence for good.

Why Is This Held ?

DVAM is an opportunity for all – victims , perpetrators, allies, supporters and elected leaders – to come together in our efforts to end domestic violence. This is a time of unity and encouragement, and of sharing their stories with victims and survivors. With so many people talking about domestic violence in a unified voice during the month of October, that raises our collective awareness of this critical issue. It is only through teamwork that domestic violence will end.

Measures By Government Women Safety

It was noted that highest abuse of women occurs on public transport. Indian Railways data showed that the number of cases of rape rose from 7 in 2016 to 10 in 2017 and 15 in 2018, although other cases of attack and molestation experienced a decline from 2016 to 2017, but were on the rise again after 2018. The government has initiated a range of initiatives for the protection and well-being of women across cities as well as remote areas of the country to strengthen this situation at the earliest opportunity.

First of all, there has been a significant increase in the number of women joining the Indian police forces, which has helped to instill a certain sense of security among many women, particularly the young working segment, as they are the ones who opt more frequently for public transport, even after peak hours on several occasions. A few of these female cops were trained exclusively for public transportation, along with CCTV cameras being mounted at train stations and other important public places. In addition there are separate desks at police stations with female officers to attend to women's complaints.

Laws Implemented : The Indian judiciary has created safety laws for women that have been effective for many years now. These include Child Marriage Restraint Act 1929, Special Marriage Act 1954, Hindu Marriage Act 1955, Hindu Widows Remarriage Act 1856, Indian Penal Code 1860, Dowry Prohibition Act 1961, Maternity Benefit Act 1861, Foreign Marriage Act 1969, Indian Divorce Act 1969, Medical Termination of Pregnancy Act 1971, Christian Marriage Act 1872, Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, Equal Remuneration Act 1976, Married Women’s Property Act 1874, Births, Deaths & Marriages Registration Act 1886, Indecent Representation of Women (Prevention) Act 1986, Muslim women (protection of rights on divorce) Act 1986, Commission of Sati (Prevention) Act 1987, National Commission for Women Act 1990, Prohibition of Sex Selection Act 1994, Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act 2005, Prevention of Children from Sexual Offences Act 2012, and Sexual Harassment of Women at Work Place Act 2013, among several others.

Technology For The Greater Good

As technology takes over the world with every passing day, with developed countries leveraging it, India is also increasingly embracing and taking advantage of the benefits it brings with it, particularly in terms of ensuring women's safety across the region.

For instance, many states have launched and are currently running the emergency helpline numbers 181 and 112 to provide assistance to women in distress, whether they are in a public location, at home or even in their workplace. These dedicated numbers are 24X7 operating, are toll-free and immediately accessible to women, and have centralized call centers that work round the clock to provide legal or police assistance, as well as medical services. Ziqitza Healthcare Limited has also operated 181 Police Helpline in Punjab to provide timely assistance to the caller in need. Set up in association with Punjab Police and the Government of Punjab, the 181 helpline service handles over 1,00,000 calls in a year, related to all kinds of harassment cases like domestic violence, rape, acid attacks, and more.

Given that travel safety has become a major concern for women, there are GPS trackers that women can use, particularly those belonging to the working class, to keep their close and dear ones in the loop of their whereabouts, especially while traveling late in the evening. In addition, smart navigation through applications such as Uber and Ola, which work closely with the police, helps to ensure the safety of female passengers and drivers. These systems have updated user-friendly features and response teams, including a zero tolerance policy on discrimination and inequality against women.

In addition to current programs, facilities and features, the Government has also proposed potential solutions to ensure women's safety and well-being in India, regardless of time and location. The Ministry of Women and Child Development (WCD) is already in the process of launching a panic button (designed in 2015) on mobile phones, as an emergency response mechanism for women across 14 country states. In addition, the Maharashtra government is also expected to include a GPS and panic button for affordable necklaces to support women in distress and emergencies.

Although India still has a long way to go to be recognized as a safe country for women, the government's sincere efforts, such as the number of emergency support lines 181 and 112, mobile apps, and a substantial increase in female police workforce among other initiatives, are definitely beginning to achieve this end objective.
