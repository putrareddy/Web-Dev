Communication

Any healthy relationship should be part of free, honest communication. The recommendations below will help to open communication channels between your partner and you. When you are in a relationship that is unhealthy or abusive, using those tips with caution. You know best about your relationship. If any of these tips put you in danger, we 're not suggesting you try them out.

For healthier communication, try to:

Find The Right Time

When you are having a serious talk with your partner about something that would bother you, it's a good idea to pick the right time to chat. Consider finding a moment when you and your partner are quiet, not upset, anxious or in a rush. When one or both of you is very busy, you might even suggest arranging a time to chat.

Talk Face To Face

Stop discussing difficult issues or questions in prose. You may misunderstand the text messages, letters and emails. So long as you feel confident to do so, speak openly so there are no needless miscommunications.

Do Not Attack

Even if we mean well, because of our word choices we can sometimes come across as harsh. It can sound like you're threatening using "you," which may make your partner defensive and less open to your message. Alternatively, consider using "I" or "we." Say, for instance, "I feel like we haven't been so close lately" instead of "You've been away from me."

Be Honest

The reality hurts sometimes but that is the secret to a healthy relationship. Admit that when you make a mistake instead of making excuses, you 're not always flawless and apologize. You'll feel happier and that's going to help improve the friendship.

Check Your Body Language

When talking, make eye contact. Just sit down and face your partner. Let it know your partner is listening. Tell them just what you care for. Do not take a phone call, email or be interrupted when talking.

Use The 48-Hour Rule

When your partner does something that makes you upset, then it's important to talk about it to them. However you don't need to do that immediately; it can be helpful to take a little time to analyze how you feel and respond in a safe way. If 48 hours later, you are still hurt, say something (and try using the tips on this page!). If not, then consider forgetting. Yet note, there is no way your partner will read your mind. But when you're angry you don't speak up, there's no way they can apologize or change their behaviour. When you state honestly apologies for your hurt feelings and your friend, let it go. When they aren't important, don't bring up past problems.

How To Communicate If You're Angry

At some point in a relationship everyone gets mad. What is crucial is that, even when frustrated, you and your partner are able to settle disagreements in a healthy way. When your partner gets upset, here are a few steps you can take:

Stop

Stop, take a step back and breathe, if you are really angry about something. Giving yourself time to relax by watching TV, talking to a friend, going for a walk, listening to some music or something that makes you relax. Having a break will prevent the situation from getting worse — and help you decide what to do to make things better.

Think

Talk about the situation when you're not agitated any more, and why you got so mad. Is it like your friend is talking or they did something? Find the real issue, and then think how to describe your feelings.

Talk

Speak to your partner, at last. Follow the Safe Communication suggestions above when you do.

Listen

Try to stop talking after you tell your partner how you feel, and listen to what they have to say. You both deserve a chance to express your feelings in a safe and healthy environment.

Communication is not always simple. Many of these tips can sound uncomfortable or awkward at first, but they will help you better connect and develop a healthier relationship.
