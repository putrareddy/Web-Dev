Tech & Social Media Safety

Technology is continuously evolving, and it can be used to threaten your health, or as a way of keeping you safe. Since issues of power and control are part of domestic violence, violent spouses also use technology to track and regulate those they harass. Below are a few points to help keep in mind when using the device.

Would you have the feeling you are being monitored? Below are some interesting stuff.

  • Are you aware that someone might control the use of another person's machine without the user knowing it?
  • Knew you couldn't delete a "past" from a machine altogether?
  • Do you know that you can mount a global positioning system ( GPS) on your vehicle, in your purse or on your mobile phone?
  • Do you know that some of the court systems placed court documents online and can contain personal information?
  • Were you aware that e-mail is like a postcard and that it can be intercepted?

There Are Ways to Guarantee Your Health

Technology is a valuable resource for those fleeing a domestic abuse situation and our hotline advocates will help you prepare to use all facets of technology safely (whether you are a survivor, friend or family member).

INTERNET SAFETY

When you browse the internet on your phone, the places that you visit are registered on the phone you are using. Bills you pay are monitored, and transactions you make. You will get text messages and emails. Bear in mind that this could be tracked when you use a computer. You can find secure computers at the nearest library, internet café, home, work center or computer technology centre. When researching topics like travel plans, housing choices, legal problems and safety plans, do use secure computers.

SOCIAL MEDIA

Only post things that you want the public to see or learn. Once it's online it isn't under your control anymore. Be protective of your personal data. Your phone numbers and addresses allow people to contact you directly, and details like your date of birth, the schools you attended, your employer and iconic pictures will make it easier for anyone to find where you work, hang out or go to school. Set limits and boundaries. Say people don't share personal information, derogatory remarks or social media check-ins about you. Tell people not to share or tag photos if they don't feel comfortable with it.

MOBILE

Can be a beacon for cell phones, tracking your exact location in real time. The abusive spouse can also access the call and email history. In addition, you should put a location tracking device (GPS) on your car or in your pocket. Consider buying a pay as you go by phone to keep you in a safe place to let you make calls.

EMAIL

This could give your abusive partner access to your email address. To be protected, open an email account on a private device your partner doesn't know about and use that account for security planning and confidential communications. It's a smart idea to keep your tracked account working with non-critical emails to maintain the looks.
