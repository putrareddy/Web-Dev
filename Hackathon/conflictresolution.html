Healthy Conflict Resolution

Conflicts, such as verbal disagreement and conflict, exist in any healthy relationship. While conflict is common, it may also be a sign that certain aspects of your relationship are not working. Healthy communication skills are essential to a peaceful resolution of conflicts. The tips below will help you overcome these claims in a balanced manner when conflict arises:

Set Boundaries

Everybody needs respect-even during an argument-to be handled. When your partner is insulting you, calling you names or ridiculating you, you should let them know that those actions are not perfect. If they don't stop, then walk away and tell them that after a break you can resume the conversation.

Finding Issue

Arguments typically occur when one partner 's needs or wishes are not met. Try getting to the center of the matter. When your partner appears to be vulnerable, they may just feel anxious and need your support. If you're mad because your partner doesn't take the garbage out, you may be really frustrated because you're feeling like you're doing all the work around the house. Learn to tackle the real problem, so you can escape endless struggles.

Agree To Disagree

When you can not solve a question with your friend, sometimes it is best to abandon it. For all you don't have to agree. Depend on what matters. If the issue is too significant for you to remove, and you can not agree to disagree, a compatibility problem can arise.

Compromise When Possible

Compromise is a big part of every productive partnership and dispute resolution. It's not always easy, but try to find a middle ground that will make you both feel comfortable with the outcome.

Consideration

Was this really an significant issue? Will it affect the way you both feel about one another? Were you violating your own values or beliefs? If so, it is important you feel comfortable to stand up for how you feel. If not, then maybe this is a time for compromise. Remember the claims of your friend, as well. How are they indignant? Through their point of view, what does that look like? Was it odd to get this angry on your partner? Will that normally weaken your partner? Were you inconsiderate?

Still to argue? If you are trying these tips but are still constantly arguing, consider whether the relationship is right for you both. If you wish to maintain the relationship, you may consider taking part together in therapy (but only if there is no violence in the relationship). You both deserve a happy, unconflicted relationship.

Conflict In Unhealthy Relationships

Although conflict is natural, it shouldn't turn your arguments into personal attacks and should never become violent. If you can't speak out without fear of your partner's retaliation, you may experience abuse. Read more about verbal / emotional harassment and how to draw the line between the violence and natural discord.

One sign of an abusive relationship is a partner that attempts to manipulate or dominate you. For instance, your partner gets upset, because:

  • You regularly spend time with friends or relatives, rather than spending your whole time with them?
  • They check your phone and don’t like the texts or calls you received?
  • They accuse you of cheating every time you leave the house?

When you suspect your relationship can be mischievous or abusive, call or speak with one of our advocates. Now let us support!
