In a continuum all partnerships occur from good to toxic with dysfunctional right in the middle.

Test the Scale of Relationships below to see where your relationship falls.

A stable relationship means your partner(s) and you are:

  • Communication:You speak about things frankly, and listen to each other. You share each other's views.
  • Respectful:You value each other as you are.
  • Trusting:You believe what you have to say about your friend. You don't sense the need to "prove" the trustworthiness of each other.
  • Enjoying Personal Time:You want to spend time apart, alone or with others. You value the need to take time away from each other.
  • Economic/Financial Partners:If it comes to investments you and your partner have fair say. All partners have access to the services which they need.
  • Engaging In Supportive Parenting:Both partners should provide parenting in a way they feel comfortable with. You work about the child(ren) needs together, as well as the parents' needs.
  • Making Mutual Sexual Choices:Together you comment freely about sexual and reproductive decisions. Both partners freely agree to sexual intercourse and should explore what you are and not comfortable with in a respectful manner.

You may be in an unhealthy relationship if one or more partners is:

  • Not Communicating:You fight when there are issues or you don't discuss them at all.
  • Disrespectful:One or more partners are not considerate of each other.
  • Not Trusting:One individual does not accept what the other says or is entitled to violate the privacy of the other.
  • Only Spending Time With Your Partner:The culture of your partner is the only one with which you socialise.
  • Unequal Economically:Finances are not addressed and/or only one party is believed to be responsible for the finances.
  • Pressured By The Other Into Sexual Activity:One partner at some stage puts pressure or shame on the other to have sex, or to do something sexual.

Violence happens when one partner in a relationship:

  • CommunicatesIn a negative, intimidating, disrespectful or demeaning way.
  • Mistreats The OtherOne partner is not respectful of the other's feelings , thoughts, decisions, opinions or physical security.
  • Accuses the other of cheating or having an affair when it’s not true:As a consequence, the person who charges may be physically or verbally abusing the other.
  • Denies that the abusive actions are abuse:An abusive partner can attempt to blame the other for their hurt, or make excuses for abusive behavior, or diminish abusive behaviour.
  • Exerts economic control:One party manages the cash and resource exposure. Getting an open Finance Dialog is not an option. It may involve stopping a partner from receiving an income or encouraging a partner to have access to their own money.
  • Forces sexual activity or pregnancy:One partner pressures the other to have sex, or at some stage do something they do not want to do sexually. In relationships where the risk of pregnancy is physical, one partner can force the other to become pregnant.
